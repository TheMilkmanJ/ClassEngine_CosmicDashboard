## 0. The problems
Cold dark matter's small-scale trouble: simulated halos are CUSPY where dwarf galaxies
show CORES (core-cusp); simulations overproduce satellites (missing satellites);
too-big-to-fail. Baryonic feedback may fix them — or the dark sector is not cold and
pointlike.

## 1. The two length scales, and what the model does NOT claim
The sector carries two derived lengths, built from different parameters, two orders apart:
the ghost-condensate **braiding scale** $r_c \sim M_{\rm Pl}/M_2^2 \approx 0.87$ kpc (built
from M₂ — the same parameter that prices dark energy, so the α_c chain grades both), and the
**soliton core** from the ultralight mass — **7 pc in a dwarf, 0.7 pc in a Milky-Way-class
halo** ([PRTOE_galactic_atoms.md](PRTOE_galactic_atoms.md)), with the de Broglie granule
scale in a dwarf at ~18 pc for company. The ground state is parsec-scale, so the model does
**not** produce kiloparsec dwarf cores — core-cusp is not this model's conquest; baryonic
feedback keeps that case. Its native small-scale claims are the smoothness floor below and
the fact that halos look cold everywhere current data reaches — which is why it survived
where lighter fuzzy-dark-matter candidates were excluded.

## 2. The floor below
The smoothness floor (the Assembly-Six family): NO dark substructure below ~10⁻³ pc —
a sharp, falsifiable anti-CDM prediction (microlensing/stream-gap surveys are the
referee). CDM predicts subhalos all the way down; the fluid forbids them.

## 3. Status and kills
The braiding scale rides M₂ and is triangulated with dark energy and 3α (the CC file), so the
α_c chain that grades the vacuum grades it too; it is not a dwarf-core claim (§1). Kills:
confirmed dark substructure below the ~10⁻³ pc floor; M₂'s triangle failing. (The soliton
scale's own kills — core radii off the 1/M_halo scaling — live in the galactic-atoms file.)
