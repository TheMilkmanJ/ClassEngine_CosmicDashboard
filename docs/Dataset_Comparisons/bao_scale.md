# The BAO standard ruler

*The model's isotropic BAO distance D_V/r_d tracks the galaxy-survey measurements with χ² = 8.8 over
six points, r_d = 145.2 Mpc. Code: `scripts/bao_scale.py`; figure `chains/bao_scale.png`.*

## The measurement

The baryon acoustic oscillation gives a standard ruler of comoving length r_d, the sound horizon at
the baryon drag epoch, imprinted on the galaxy distribution. The surveys measure the ratio D_V/r_d at
their effective redshifts: 6dFGS (z = 0.106), SDSS MGS (z = 0.15), BOSS DR12 (z = 0.38, 0.51, 0.61),
and eBOSS QSO (z = 1.48).

## The model

The model's sound horizon at drag is r_d = 145.2 Mpc, against ΛCDM's 147.1 Mpc — the varying-mₑ
recombination shift lowers it by ~1.2%. Its D_V/r_d(z) runs through the six survey points with
χ² = 8.8, comparable to ΛCDM's fit, so the model reproduces the standard-ruler distance–redshift
relation across the range the surveys cover.
