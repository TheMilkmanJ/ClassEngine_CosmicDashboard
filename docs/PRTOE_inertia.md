## 1. The question nobody bills

Newton's first law is an axiom in classical mechanics, a geodesic postulate in general
relativity, and an unexamined inheritance everywhere else: **why does coasting cost
nothing?** Any theory that fills space with a physical medium owes an answer, because
media drag. This objection — not interferometry — was the aether's first and oldest
problem: a planet plowing through a substance for 10⁹ years should decelerate, heat, and
spiral. It doesn't. Empty space became "empty" largely because nobody could build a
medium that charges nothing for passage.

## 2. The one loophole, and the model's identity

Condensed matter found the loophole in 1941: **a superfluid is the unique state of
matter through which sub-critical motion is exactly dissipation-free.** Landau's
criterion: an object moving at velocity v through a condensate can shed energy only if
some excitation of the medium satisfies

$$\varepsilon(\mathbf{p}) - \mathbf{v}\cdot\mathbf{p} < 0, \qquad v_c = \min_{\mathbf{p}}\,\frac{\varepsilon(\mathbf{p})}{|\mathbf{p}|}.$$

For a linear (phonon-class)
dispersion ε = c_s|p|, no excitation qualifies until v > c_s — below that threshold the
medium *cannot accept* the object's momentum, and drag is not small but **zero**. This
is laboratory fact (superfluid ⁴He, ³He, dilute-gas condensates), not analogy.

This model's founding identity is that the vacuum *is* such a condensate, and its
phonon-class excitations ride the light cone: the excitation metric's limiting speed is
c (the one-metric structure; the special-relativity file's trunk claim). Therefore:

**The Landau critical velocity of the vacuum is c. Below it, the medium charges nothing.
Newton's first law is not an axiom here — it is the superfluid's zero-drag certificate.**

Free motion exists because the one medium that survived 1887 is the one medium that
never drags.

## 4. The speed limit gets a mechanism

In fundamental relativity, c is a wall by decree. Here it is a **threshold with
physics on both sides**:

- **Below the cone**: dissipation-free passage, at any speed, forever. The medium is
 invisible to a coasting body not because it is absent but because a condensate has
 no state to sell it.
- **At the cone**: the Landau criterion saturates. A super-critical object would shed
 quasiparticles into the vacuum — Cherenkov radiation with the vacuum itself as the
 dielectric. "Breaking yourself" is literal: the medium starts billing, and the bill
 is paid in the traveler's own kinetic energy and structure.
- **The enforcement is a ramp, not a wall** (the depth law, applied): the threshold is
 approached asymptotically because the energy cost of acceleration diverges smoothly
 as v → c (the relativistic kinetic-energy ramp, inherited exactly from the emergent
 metric), and above-threshold dissipation would itself switch on continuously
 (Cherenkov-class power grows smoothly from zero at threshold — excitation phase
 space, not a step). No step anywhere; the limit is priced, not decreed.

**The passed exam this already implies**: if the vacuum's dispersion bent *downward*
at high momentum (a roton-class minimum, as in ⁴He), the critical velocity would sit
*below* c and ultra-relativistic particles would shed vacuum quasiparticles in flight.
Ultra-high-energy cosmic rays crossing ~Gpc distances without such losses
[Coleman–Glashow 1999-class bounds] therefore test the dispersion's linearity — and
the model's microstructure scale is *forced* to Planck (the basement's ruling), putting
its predicted deviations at (E/M_Pl)ⁿ: below current reach, same envelope as the
Lorentz-violation budget. One suppression meets both requirements — a consistency, not a
coincidence.

(i) **Vacuum drag**: any confirmed anomalous secular deceleration of a coasting body
attributable to the vacuum (precision orbiter/pulsar-binary tracking are the standing
