## 1. The claim, stated precisely

In this model there is no "quantize gravity" problem, because there is no fundamental
metric to quantize. Spacetime geometry is the long-wavelength collective description of
a cosmological superfluid condensate, and the condensate is a quantum system from the
outset. Gravity is therefore quantum *by inheritance* — in the same sense that the
hydrodynamics of superfluid helium requires no independent quantization once the
underlying atoms are quantum. This is the standard analog-gravity position (Volovik;
Barceló–Liberati–Visser), here assembled with the model's own field content and pushed
to a quantitative endpoint: **a closed one-loop expression for Newton's constant, with
one measured coupling standing between the framework and verdict.**

## 2. Three routes to the emergent metric

Three independent constructions, each established within the model's production
framework, yield the same effective metric:

1. **The acoustic metric.** Low-energy excitations of the condensate propagate on an
 effective geometry g_μν built from the condensate's density and flow (the standard
 phase-EFT isomorphism). Horizons are sonic horizons; geodesics are quasiparticle
 trajectories.
2. **Sakharov induction** [Sakharov 1967]. The Einstein–Hilbert term is generated by
 the one-loop fluctuations of the fields living in the condensate: the gravitational
 stiffness M² is a calculable output, not an input. Section 5 carries this route to
 its closed form.
3. **The thermodynamic route** [Jacobson 1995]. Einstein's equations follow as an
 equation of state from the Clausius relation δQ = T dS applied to local horizons.
 Each input Jacobson requires was derived independently within the model before the
 assembly was recognized: the work term dE = −p dV, the
 Hamiltonian constraint, the dissipative bookkeeping, and the area law (originally
 imported; see §4, item 2).

## 3. Quantum consistency by inheritance

The condensate's canonical pair is particle number and phase, [θ̂, N̂] = i (Anderson's
commutator) — the same algebraic structure that protects the f-sum rule. The emergent
metric is a composite of these canonically quantized operators, so metric perturbations
(the collective mode playing the role of the graviton) are quantum without any separate
quantization step. Gravitational and electromagnetic signals propagate on the same
effective cone by construction, a property already tested by the GW170817 multimessenger
timing and passed identically.
