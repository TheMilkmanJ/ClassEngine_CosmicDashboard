# The comoving genesis solver — Ψ₀ and f_amp from one field roll

> **What this is.** A comoving Affleck–Dine field-roll solver for the genesis epoch:
> the complex order parameter Ψ = R·e^{iθ} evolved on the recorded potential in a
> radiation-era background a(t) ∝ √t with Hubble friction H = 1/(2t), from the frozen
> release through oscillation onset into the redshifting regime. Code:
> `scripts/genesis_solver_B1.py`. Closed-form anchors added to
> `scripts/audit_math_pass.py` (5 checks, all pass). It is the superset of the four
> genesis half-solvers — `genesis_famp_Z4.py` (the zero-mode roll), `genesis_multicomponent.py`
> (the winding channels), `genesis_joint_draw.py` (winding and rotation on one ring),
> and `granule_sim_2field.py` (the late-universe contrast) — carrying the comoving
> background and the abundance closure that none of them held, and validated to reduce
> to each where they overlap.

## The potential and the background

The field rolls on the recorded genesis potential

  V = m²R² + λR⁴ + 2 ε_A λ R⁴ cos4θ ,  ε_A = 2/9 ,

released **at rest** at a release phase θ_i drawn from the uniform post-inflation
prior. The Z₄ tilt (period π/2 in θ) torques the field as it rolls, so any rotation is
generated by the dynamics, not put in by hand. In code units m = 1, λ = 0.03 the
quartic-to-mass crossover sits at R_× = √(m²/λ) = 5.77 and the release radius is
R_i = 10, i.e. R_i/R_× = 1.73 — released just above the crossover.

## f_amp — the amplitude-mode fraction (the genesis dice)

The late-time orbit is an ellipse in the complex plane; its energy splits between the
radial breathing mode (the amplitude |Ψ| oscillation, which is what a mass rides on)
and the phase rotation (the U(1) mode, which carries charge). f_amp is the radial
fraction, ⟨E_rad⟩/⟨E_rad + E_rot⟩, averaged over the late window. It sets the granule
density contrast through S = (1 + (1 − f_amp)²)/2.

f_amp is **distributional** — one draw per universe, set by the release phase. Over the
uniform θ_i prior at the recorded tilt ε_A = 2/9:

| tilt ε_A | median f_amp | band |
|---|---|---|
| 0.10 | 0.59 | [0.27, 1.00] |
| 2/9 | **0.63** | [0.04, 1.00] |
| 0.30 | 0.73 | [0.15, 1.00] |

At the recorded tilt the median is **f_amp ≈ 0.63**, band [0.19, 0.87] (16th–84th
percentile). The granule-contrast cross-check reads S = 0.57 at the median, matching
the two-fluid meter's 0.55–0.60. The value slides gently with the tilt strength; the
mechanism (orbit eccentricity → radial fraction) and the ≈0.6 centre are robust. The
phase where the tilt vanishes (θ_i at a cos4θ extremum) gives a purely radial orbit,
f_amp → 1, which is why the band reaches the upper edge.

## Ψ₀ — the genesis amplitude (the misalignment abundance closure)

Once the field oscillates quadratically the comoving energy density ρ·a³ is an
adiabatic invariant and freezes. Matching that frozen density to the measured
dark-matter abundance pins Ψ₀:

  ρ_DM,0 = ½ m² Ψ₀² a_osc³ ,  a_osc from H = H₀√Ω_r a⁻² = m .

This closes **analytically** on the recorded inputs — ρ_DM,0 (from Ω_DM, H₀, M_red) and
the measured mass m = 2.24×10⁻²⁰ eV — with no simulation in the loop:

  **Ψ₀ = 5.0×10¹⁶ GeV = 2.07% M_red** ,  z_osc = 4.0×10⁷ ,  Ψ₀ ∝ m^(−1/4) .

The scaling is exact: a_osc ∝ m^(−1/2) in the radiation era gives Ψ₀² ∝ ρ_DM,0/(m² a_osc³) ∝ m^(−1/2),
and a log-log fit of the closure returns slope −0.2500 across three decades of m.

Two independent readings of Ψ₀ coincide: the misalignment amplitude (5.03×10¹⁶ GeV) and
the potential's own quartic-to-mass crossover m/√λ at the recorded λ ≈ 2×10⁻⁹¹
(5.01×10¹⁶ GeV) agree to 0.4%. So the genesis field is released **at** the crossover:
the roll is near-quadratic from the start, the anharmonic correction to the abundance
is small, and the leading closure is robust.

## What the comoving roll adds beyond the analytic closure

The closure assumes the misalignment mechanism; the roll checks it and prices its one
correction:

- **The mechanism is realised.** ρ·a³ rises during the frozen phase and then plateaus
  once the field is quadratic-oscillating; the late-window flatness is 0.9%. The
  comoving density genuinely freezes.
- **The onset+anharmonic correction is O(1) and scale-free.** The frozen abundance sits
  a factor 𝒜 = 1.78 above the sharp-onset estimate the closure uses. This factor is
  identical under the rescaling m² → 4m², R_i → 2R_i, t → t/2 (the dimensionless roll is
  unchanged), so it is m-independent and the m^(−1/4) scaling survives it exactly.
  Folding it in shifts Ψ₀ by ∼1/√𝒜 = 0.75× to ≈3.8×10¹⁶ GeV — an O(1) move that keeps
  the amplitude at 10¹⁶ GeV. Isolating the quartic (full roll vs a quadratic-only roll
  at the same amplitude) shows it bleeds the abundance to 0.73× the harmonic value, the
  expected radiation-like redshift while quartic-dominated.

## Cross-validation against the four half-solvers

- **The zero-mode roll.** The vectorised ring at N = 1 reproduces the reference
  integrator's f_amp to |Δ| = 0.001, and the generated charge splits evenly over one
  Z₄ period (12 positive / 12 negative) with the reflection antisymmetry
  Q(θ) = −Q(π/2 − θ) holding to 6×10⁻¹⁵ — neither sign of rotation is preferred a priori.
- **The winding channels.** A clean n-ramp returns exact integer winding for
  n = −3…3; the coherent fraction |⟨e^{iθ}⟩| matches its channel definition and drops
  under a half-quantum-vortex offset; the winding counter agrees with the joint-draw
  instrument.
- **The comoving ring.** At N = 1 it matches the zero-mode roll (|Δ| = 0.005); at N > 1
  it carries an integer winding through the full comoving history.
- **The abundance closure** ties back to the analytic backbone at 5.03×10¹⁶ GeV and to
  the granule contrast at S ≈ 0.57.

All four validations pass; the run is single-threaded on a tiny grid, sub-minute, with
a production configuration written but not called.

## Status

Both objects are delivered as numbers, and neither is gated on a production run:

- **Ψ₀ = 5.0×10¹⁶ GeV** — fixed by the misalignment abundance closure on the recorded
  (ρ_DM,0, m), ∝ m^(−1/4). The comoving roll confirms the mechanism and shows the one
  correction is a scale-free O(1) that leaves the amplitude and its scaling intact. The
  absolute normalization is analytic, not sim-gated.
- **f_amp = 0.63, band [0.19, 0.87]** — the genesis dice at the recorded tilt,
  converged on a tiny grid; one draw per universe.

What remains genuinely sim- or draw-gated is elsewhere: the winding integer of our own
universe is a genesis draw (its input is unset in the corpus, not computable from the
potential), and the hydrodynamic side of the genesis build — the ring-formation
circulation, the discharge geometry, the flow's coherent fraction — is a separate
solver track. Neither bears on Ψ₀ or f_amp, which stand as computed here.
