# The 3.5×10¹⁷ GeV "portal scale" — appears in two files and nowhere else

**Your call needed.** I corrected the downstream reasoning but did not delete the number,
because I cannot tell whether it is a dead relic or a real object wearing the wrong name.

## What I found

`PRTOE_direct_detection.md` and `PRTOE_indirect_detection.md` both justify their nulls with
"the portal at ~3.5×10¹⁷ GeV (geometric mean)". That figure appears **nowhere else in the
corpus** — no derivation, no registry entry, no dependency-tree row.

Every other portal number the model carries is twelve or more orders below it:

| object | scale | where |
|---|---|---|
| the anchor | 1.57 TeV | hierarchy problem |
| the census portal | 13–20 TeV | two-loop shooter |
| the dyad's decay constant f | 100–500 TeV | the standing configuration |
| **"the portal" in these two files** | **3.5×10¹⁷ GeV** | **only these two files** |

## Why it matters

`direct_detection` uses it to declare collider missing-energy **NULL**. But the referee calendar
lists HL-LHC as a *live* referee on "the portal's visible branch (13–20 TeV at two loops —
currently beyond reach; the edge audit may move it)". So one document says colliders can never
see the portal because it sits at 10¹⁷ GeV, while another schedules HL-LHC to look for it.

## Three possibilities

1. **A dead relic** from an earlier configuration — delete it and the null rests on the TeV
   scales being beyond reach (which they are, so the conclusion survives).
2. **A different object mislabelled** — 10¹⁷ GeV is seesaw/GUT territory, so this may be the
   heavy Majorana scale or an induced-gravity scale that got called "the portal."
3. **Something real and underived** that should be sourced properly.

## What I changed

I rewrote the *reasoning* in both files to rest on the sourced scales, so no audience-facing
claim now depends on the unsourced number. The number itself is untouched pending your ruling.
